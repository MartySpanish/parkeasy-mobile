Parking in Ballycastle

Ballycastle is a small seaside town on the Causeway Coast, so most parking sits within a short walk of the seafront, the marina and The Diamond at the top of the town. The largest and handiest car parks are along the seafront near the beach and harbour, with further spaces around the marina where the ferry to Rathlin Island departs. These fill up fastest on summer weekends and sunny days, so arriving earlier in the morning makes a real difference.

On-street parking is available along Quay Road, Mary Street, Castle Street and around The Diamond, with much of it free but time-limited by disc or signed restrictions, so always check the signs before leaving your car. For day trips, the seafront and marina car parks are usually the most reliable bet, while the streets up around The Diamond suit shorter stops for the shops, cafes and the Marconi monument area.

Ballycastle parking FAQs

Is there free parking in Ballycastle?

Yes. Much of the on-street parking around The Diamond, Castle Street and the seafront area is free, though some bays are time-limited, so check the signs. The seafront and marina car parks are generally the most convenient free spaces for visitors heading to the beach or the Rathlin ferry.

What about parking on Sundays and in the evenings?

Time limits and disc restrictions are usually relaxed in the evenings and on Sundays, so parking is typically easier and freer then. The seafront and marina remain the best places to leave your car, but always read the local signage as some restrictions can still apply during busy periods.

Where should I avoid parking, especially during the Auld Lammas Fair?

During the Auld Lammas Fair in late August the town centre and seafront get extremely busy, with road closures around The Diamond, Castle Street and Quay Road. Avoid central streets on fair days and use park-and-walk options or arrive very early, as nearby spaces disappear quickly.
