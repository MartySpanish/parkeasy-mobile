Parking in Omagh

Omagh is the main town of County Tyrone, and most drivers head for the centre around High Street, Market Street and John Street. The town has a mix of council-run pay-and-display car parks and some on-street bays, so you rarely have to circle for long. Handy central options include the car park near the Strule Arts Centre on Townhall Square, parking around the Showgrounds, and bays near the courthouse and the bus centre off Mountjoy Road. For shopping runs, the retail areas out toward the Showgrounds and the larger supermarkets on the edge of town offer easy, free customer parking.

Pay-and-display charges in the central council car parks are generally modest and apply during weekday daytime hours, with tariffs shown on the machines. Many spaces become free in the evenings and on Sundays, which makes nights out and weekend visits straightforward. If you are visiting the Ulster American Folk Park just north of town at Castletown, there is dedicated free visitor parking on site. As always, check the signs on the day, since tariffs and restrictions can change.

Omagh parking FAQs

Is there any free parking in Omagh?

Yes. The large supermarkets and retail areas on the edge of town offer free customer parking, and the Ulster American Folk Park at Castletown has free visitor parking. Many central pay-and-display car parks also become free in the evenings and on Sundays, but always check the sign at the entrance before you leave the car.

Do I have to pay for parking on Sundays and in the evenings?

In most of Omagh's council pay-and-display car parks, charges apply during weekday daytime hours only, so parking is typically free in the evenings and on Sundays. Restrictions can vary from one car park to the next, so read the tariff board at the entrance to be sure for that specific location.

Where should I avoid parking in Omagh town centre?

Avoid loading bays and disabled bays unless you are entitled to use them, and watch the on-street bays along High Street and Market Street, which often have short time limits to keep spaces turning over for shoppers. For a longer stay it is usually easier and cheaper to use one of the council car parks just off the main streets rather than fight for a kerbside space.
