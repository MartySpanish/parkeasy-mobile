Parking in Banbridge

Banbridge sits on the main A1 route between Belfast and Newry, and most parking in the town centre is clustered around the steep main thoroughfare known locally as The Cut on Newry Street and Bridge Street. The town is well served by free council-run car parks, including the large Bridge Street car park behind the main street, the Townhall area, and parking around the Castlewellan Road end near Banbridge Health Centre. The Boulevard, the big outlet shopping centre just off the A1 on the southern edge of town, has its own extensive free car park for shoppers.

On-street parking is available along Newry Street, Bridge Street and Rathfriland Street, though these spaces are often busy on weekday mornings and Saturdays when the town is at its liveliest. Disc and limited-stay restrictions apply in some central bays, so check the signs before leaving your car. For longer visits, the off-street car parks near Bridge Street and the Old Town Hall are usually the easiest bet, and many spaces become free or quieter in the evenings and on Sundays.

Banbridge parking FAQs

Is there free parking in Banbridge town centre?

Yes. Banbridge has several free council-operated car parks, including the Bridge Street car park and parking near the Old Town Hall, both within a short walk of Newry Street and the main shops. The Boulevard outlet centre on the southern edge of town also offers free parking for visitors.

What is parking like on Sundays and in the evenings?

Banbridge is much quieter on Sundays and after shops close, so finding a space is rarely a problem. Many on-street bays and council car parks have relaxed or no charges and restrictions outside core daytime hours, but it is always worth checking the signage on the bay you choose.

Where should I avoid parking in Banbridge?

Avoid the steep central bays along The Cut on Newry Street and Bridge Street during weekday mornings and Saturday afternoons, when they fill quickly and have time limits. The hill can also be awkward for parking, so the flatter off-street car parks behind the main street are usually a calmer choice.
