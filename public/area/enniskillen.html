Parking in Enniskillen

Enniskillen is the main town of County Fermanagh, built on an island between the upper and lower sections of Lough Erne, which means the historic core around the High Street and Townhall Street can get tight, especially on market days and during the summer tourist season. Most pay-and-display car parks are run by Fermanagh and Omagh District Council, with handy options at the Erneside Shopping Centre, East Bridge Street, the Townhall area, and near the bus station. The Erneside multi-storey is the most convenient choice if you want covered parking close to the main shops, while the Lakeland Forum and Broadmeadow areas offer larger surface car parks a short walk from the centre.

If you are visiting attractions like Enniskillen Castle, the Buttermarket craft courtyard, or heading out to Castle Coole or Florence Court, it is worth parking once and exploring the compact town centre on foot, as the main shopping streets are pedestrian-friendly. On-street spaces along Belmore Street and around the Diamond are limited and time-restricted, so the council car parks are usually a safer bet for anything longer than a quick errand. Enniskillen parking FAQs

Is there free parking in Enniskillen?

Council pay-and-display car parks in Enniskillen are generally free on Sundays and after the charging period ends in the early evening (typically after 6pm on weekdays). Some outlying surface car parks and supermarket car parks, such as at the larger retail areas on the edge of town, also offer free customer parking. Always check the signage at the specific car park, as rules vary between sites.

Where should I park to visit Enniskillen town centre and the castle?

For the main shops, the Erneside Shopping Centre multi-storey or the East Bridge Street car park put you within a short walk of the High Street. For Enniskillen Castle and the Buttermarket, the car parks near the Lakeland Forum and the western end of the island are closest. From most central car parks the whole town centre is walkable in under ten minutes.

Where should I avoid parking in Enniskillen?

Avoid relying on on-street spaces along Belmore Street, Townhall Street and around the Diamond during busy weekday mornings and market days, as they are limited and time-restricted with regular enforcement. The narrow one-way system on the island can also make finding a kerbside space slow, so it is usually quicker to head straight to a council car park rather than circling the centre.
