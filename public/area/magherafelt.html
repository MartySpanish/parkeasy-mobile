Parking in Magherafelt

Magherafelt is a busy market town in Mid Ulster, and most of its parking is centred around the town centre off Broad Street, Rainey Street and Church Street. The good news for drivers is that Mid Ulster District Council operates a number of off-street car parks in the town that are free to use, including spaces around the Bridewell, Garden Street and the Meeting of the Waters area, so you rarely need to pay to leave your car for a short shop or appointment. On-street parking is available along the main streets too, though it is usually limited-waiting in the core of the town to keep spaces turning over for shoppers.

The town gets noticeably busier on its weekly market days and on weekend afternoons, when the central car parks near Broad Street and the larger stores such as the local supermarkets fill up first. If you are visiting for the leisure centre, the library or a GP appointment, the quieter car parks a short walk from the main shopping streets are often a better bet. Magherafelt parking FAQs

Is there free parking in Magherafelt?

Yes. Mid Ulster District Council provides several free off-street car parks in and around Magherafelt town centre, so you can usually park without paying for shopping or appointments. Supermarket car parks are also free for customers, typically with a maximum stay. Always check the signage at each car park, as some central spaces have limited-waiting restrictions to keep them turning over.

What is parking like on Sundays and in the evenings?

Parking is at its easiest on Sunday mornings and on weekday evenings once shops and offices close, with most central spaces freeing up. Where on-street limited-waiting restrictions apply, they generally only operate during normal daytime business hours, so evenings and Sundays are usually unrestricted. Check the local signage to be sure, as bays near pubs and restaurants can still fill up on busy nights.

Where should I avoid parking in Magherafelt on a busy day?

On market days and Saturday afternoons, the on-street bays and car parks right on Broad Street and around the main supermarkets are the first to fill and the slowest to find a space in. If it's busy, head for one of the council car parks a couple of minutes' walk from the main shopping streets instead, and check ParkEasy first to see where spaces are actually available.
