Parking in Bangor

Bangor sits on the County Down coast just along the Belfast Lough, and most visitors head for the seafront, Main Street and the marina area. Ards and North Down Borough Council runs several pay-and-display car parks close to the town centre, including the large Queen's Parade and seafront car parks near Pickie Fun Park and the marina, plus smaller lots off Central Avenue and Quay Street. These fill up quickly on sunny weekends and during events like the seaside festivals, so arriving early or trying the slightly cheaper outer car parks is usually the smarter move.

If you are shopping, the Bloomfield Shopping Centre and the nearby Bloomfield retail parks on the edge of town offer large free customer car parks, which is why many locals drive there rather than circling the centre. For the train and bus, the Bangor integrated transport hub on Abbey Street has parking nearby, and on-street pay-and-display bays run along streets such as High Street, Main Street and Hamilton Road. Bangor parking FAQs

Is there any free parking in Bangor?

Yes. The biggest free options are the customer car parks at Bloomfield Shopping Centre and the Bloomfield retail parks on the southern edge of town, which are popular with locals doing a longer shop. In the centre itself, most council car parks and on-street bays are pay-and-display during the day, though some residential side streets away from Main Street and the seafront offer free on-street spaces if you are willing to walk a few minutes. Always check the signage, as free stretches can be time-limited or residents-only.

Do I have to pay for parking on Sundays and in the evenings?

Council pay-and-display car parks and on-street bays in Bangor are generally chargeable during daytime hours, but charges typically stop in the evening and many bays are free on Sundays. Times vary by car park, so always read the tariff board before leaving your car. The seafront and marina car parks can still be extremely busy on warm Sunday afternoons even when parking is free, so factor in time to find a space near Pickie and the promenade.

Where should I avoid parking in Bangor on a busy day?

On sunny weekends and during seaside events, the Queen's Parade and seafront car parks near the marina and Pickie Fun Park fill first and can mean a lot of slow circling. Main Street and the immediate town-centre bays also get tight on Saturdays. If the centre looks full, head to the Bloomfield free customer car parks and walk in, or use one of the smaller outer council car parks.
