Parking in Portadown

Portadown sits at the heart of the Craigavon Borough, and most drivers head straight for the town centre around High Street, Market Street and West Street. The biggest pull is The Meadows Shopping Centre off Mandeville Street, which has a large customer car park, while Bridge Street and Edward Street offer council pay-and-display areas handy for the shops, banks and the People's Park. On-street bays around High Street and Thomas Street are convenient but tend to be short-stay and metered during the day, so check the signage before you leave the car.

If you would rather not pay, there are free options a short walk from the centre, and on-street parking restrictions ease in the evenings and on Sundays in many spots. Train and bus users can also use Portadown railway station off Watson Street, which has its own parking. ParkEasy is a free, community-powered app, so local drivers share live tips on where spaces are actually available, which car parks fill up on market days, and where the cheaper long-stay options are around town.

Portadown parking FAQs

Is there any free parking in Portadown town centre?

Yes. Several council car parks and on-street bays around the centre are free outside charging hours, and many on-street restrictions lift in the evenings and on Sundays. Always read the signs on the bay, as time limits and charging periods vary street by street. Larger retail car parks like The Meadows are free for customers while you shop.

Do I have to pay for parking in Portadown on Sundays or in the evenings?

Most council pay-and-display car parks and metered on-street bays in Portadown only charge during daytime hours on Monday to Saturday, so parking is generally free on Sunday and in the evenings. Confirm the exact times on the meter or signage before leaving your car, since a few private and retail car parks set their own rules.

Where should I avoid parking in Portadown?

Avoid leaving your car in short-stay or loading bays along High Street and Market Street if you need a few hours, as these are time-limited and enforced during the day. Town-centre spaces also get tight on market days and Saturday mornings, so for longer visits aim for a longer-stay car park or the railway station and walk in.
