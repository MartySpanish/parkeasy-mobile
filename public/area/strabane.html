Parking in Strabane

Strabane sits on the banks of the River Mourne in west County Tyrone, right on the border with Lifford in County Donegal. The good news for drivers is that parking in the town is generally easy and, in most cases, free. The main town centre car park behind the shops off Railway Street and Bowling Green offers free parking close to Main Street and Market Street, and there is usually space even on busy market days. On-street parking is available along Main Street, Castle Street and Abercorn Square, though some bays are time-limited to keep spaces turning over for shoppers.

If you are heading to the supermarkets, Asda on Branch Road and the Lidl and other retail units off Melmount Road have their own free customer car parks, which are handy for both shopping and reaching the town centre on foot. For visitors exploring the town, free or low-cost parking near the bus centre and the Alley Theatre on Railway Street makes a good base. Strabane parking FAQs

Is there free parking in Strabane town centre?

Yes. Much of Strabane's town centre parking is free, including the council car parks near Bowling Green and Railway Street, off Main Street. Supermarkets such as Asda and Lidl off Melmount and Branch Road also offer free customer parking. Always check the signs, as some on-street bays on Main Street are time-limited even when free of charge.

Can I park for free on Sundays and in the evenings?

Strabane is a small town and on-street and council car park restrictions are typically relaxed in the evenings and on Sundays, when many shops are closed. The Alley Theatre car park on Railway Street is useful for evening events. Even so, glance at the posted signs, as the safest approach is to confirm the times listed on the nearest pay or restriction notice.

Where should I avoid parking in Strabane?

Avoid parking on the narrow stretches of Main Street and around Abercorn Square during busy weekday shopping hours, where bays are limited and turnover is high. Keep clear of loading bays near the shops and double yellow lines on the approach roads. For longer stays, use the free council car parks off Railway Street and Bowling Green rather than risking a time-limited on-street bay.
