Parking in Antrim

Antrim is a compact town on the shores of Lough Neagh, and most visitors find parking straightforward compared with bigger centres like Belfast. The main town-centre options sit around the High Street and the Castle Mall (formerly Castle Centre) shopping area, with surface car parks off Church Street and Castle Way. The Castle Mall car park is the busiest and most central for the High Street shops and Antrim Courthouse, while smaller council-run car parks nearby are handy for quick errands. For a day out, Antrim Castle Gardens and Clotworthy House have their own visitor parking off Randalstown Road close to the Lough.

If you are heading to the retail side of town rather than the centre, Junction One International Outlet on the edge of Antrim near the M2 and A26 offers large free car parks, as do the main supermarkets such as Tesco and Sainsbury's. For events at the Antrim Forum leisure centre, plan to arrive early as its car park fills quickly on match and gym-class days. ParkEasy is free and community-powered, so locals share up-to-date notes on which Antrim car parks are free, where on-street bays carry time limits, and where you are most likely to find a space at busy times.

Antrim parking FAQs

Is there free parking in Antrim town centre?

Yes. Several council-run car parks around the High Street and town centre offer free parking, though many have a time limit such as two or three hours during the day, so always check the signs on arrival. The big retail destinations on the edge of town, including the Junction One outlet and supermarkets like Tesco and Sainsbury's, also provide free car parks for customers.

What is parking like on Sundays and in the evenings?

On-street bays and most council car parks in Antrim are generally free or have relaxed restrictions on Sundays and in the evenings, which makes the town centre much easier to park in outside normal shopping hours. It is still worth reading the signage on each street, as a few bays keep their limits, and parking near the Antrim Forum or any evening event venue can be tight when something is on.

Where should I avoid parking in Antrim?

Avoid leaving a car over its posted time limit in the short-stay bays around the High Street and Castle Mall, as these are intended for quick turnover and are checked. Steer clear of blocking residential streets near the town centre and Lough shore, and if you are visiting the Castle Gardens or Forum during a busy event, use the dedicated visitor or leisure car parks rather than circling the narrow streets nearby.
