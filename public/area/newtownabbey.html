Parking in Newtownabbey

Newtownabbey is a large, spread-out town on the northern edge of Belfast, so parking really depends on which part you are heading to. Most people aiming for the shops use the Abbeycentre on Longwood Road, which has a big free customer car park, while Glengormley village along the Antrim Road and the surrounding districts each have their own mix of small car parks and on-street spaces. Because the town is made up of several connected areas, including Whiteabbey, Carnmoney, Mossley and Rathcoole, it pays to plan around your exact destination rather than the town as a whole.

Unlike Belfast city centre, much of Newtownabbey offers free parking, especially at retail and supermarket sites and in residential areas, which makes it an easier place to drive than the city. The main things to watch are the busy spells around the Abbeycentre at weekends and in the run-up to Christmas, restricted residential streets near Glengormley where double yellow lines and bus stops are common, and the park-and-ride options that serve commuters heading into Belfast. Newtownabbey parking FAQs

Is there free parking in Newtownabbey?

Yes, a lot of parking in Newtownabbey is free. The Abbeycentre on Longwood Road has a large free customer car park, and the big supermarkets in the area generally offer free parking for shoppers. Many residential streets in Glengormley, Whiteabbey and Carnmoney also have free on-street parking, though you should always check for yellow lines, bus stops and resident restrictions before leaving your car.

Where should I avoid parking around Glengormley?

Glengormley village along the Antrim Road gets very busy and the side streets near the shops, schools and churches often have double yellow lines, short waiting limits or bus stops. Avoid blocking residential driveways and parking on the main Antrim Road during peak times. If the village is full, it is usually quicker to use a nearby supermarket or retail car park and walk in than to circle for an on-street space.

What is parking like on Sundays and in the evenings?

Evenings and Sundays are generally the easiest times to park in Newtownabbey. The Abbeycentre and supermarket car parks are quieter outside core shopping hours, and on-street parking around Glengormley and the residential areas frees up considerably. Many time-limited on-street restrictions ease in the evenings, but it is still worth reading the signs on the street since rules can vary spot to spot.
