Parking in Downpatrick

Downpatrick, the County Down town long linked with Saint Patrick, sees its busiest parking around Market Street, Irish Street and the shops near the bottom of the town hill. Newry, Mourne and Down District Council runs several public car parks here, and many of them are free, which makes parking noticeably easier than in a lot of Northern Ireland towns. Handy options sit close to the Market Street shops, the supermarkets, the leisure centre and the cathedral end of town near the Saint Patrick Centre.

On-street parking along Market Street and the main shopping streets is usually limited-wait, so check the signs and avoid leaving a car there all day. For longer visits, including Down Cathedral, the Saint Patrick Centre, the Down County Museum or the Downpatrick and County Down Railway, use a dedicated car park rather than the kerb. Downpatrick parking FAQs

Is there free parking in Downpatrick?

Yes. Several of the council-run public car parks in Downpatrick offer free parking, which is one of the town's real advantages over bigger centres. The car parks around the Market Street area are usually your best bet. Always check the sign on arrival, as some free spaces are still time-limited, and supermarket car parks such as Lidl or SuperValu are for customers and have their own limits.

What is parking like on Sundays and in the evenings?

Downpatrick is much quieter outside normal shop hours, so finding a space on a Sunday or in the evening is generally easy. On-street limited-wait restrictions and the busiest car parks ease off once the shops close, but if there is an event at the cathedral, the racecourse or the Saint Patrick Centre the nearest spaces can still go quickly, so arrive a little early.

Where should I avoid parking in Downpatrick?

Avoid relying on Market Street and the narrow main streets for a long stay, as these are limited-wait and turn over quickly. Steer clear of supermarket car parks if you are not shopping there, and watch for restrictions near Down Cathedral and the Saint Patrick Centre on event days. For anything more than a quick errand, head straight for a dedicated public car park.
