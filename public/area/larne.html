Parking in Larne

Larne is a busy County Antrim port town, and most short trips centre on the Main Street and Lower Cross area, the Laharna Retail Park, and the harbour where the Cairnryan ferry comes in. The town is compact, so parking is rarely a serious headache compared with Belfast, but the handiest spots near Main Street can fill up on Saturdays and around ferry sailing times. Mid and East Antrim Borough Council runs the main public car parks, and several of them offer free or low-cost short stays close to the shops.

Larne parking FAQs

Is there any free parking in Larne town centre?

Yes. The retail and supermarket car parks on the edge of town, including Laharna Retail Park and the larger store car parks off the main road, offer free parking while you shop, which is the easiest option for a quick trip. Several council bays around the town centre also have free short-stay periods, but always read the signs as time limits are enforced. For all-day stays, the council car parks near Main Street are usually the better bet.

What are parking rules like on Sundays and in the evenings?

Larne is much quieter on Sundays and after the shops close, and on-street time limits and any pay periods generally apply only during the signed hours, so evening and Sunday parking is usually straightforward and often free. Retail park spaces are open while the stores are trading. As always, check the nearest sign, since a few restricted zones still apply outside normal shopping hours.

Where should I park for the Larne ferry, and where is best avoided?

If you are sailing to Cairnryan with the ferry, follow the harbour signs and use the official port parking rather than leaving a car on town streets, which are not meant for long stays. Avoid parking tight to Main Street, the Lower Cross junction and the access roads near the port at peak times, as these get congested and are closely monitored. For a longer visit, a council car park a short walk from the centre is calmer and less likely to be ticketed.
