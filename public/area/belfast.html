Parking in Belfast

Belfast city centre is compact and well served by car parks, so most drivers find a space within a short walk of wherever they're headed. Popular multi-storey and surface options include the car parks at Victoria Square and CastleCourt off Royal Avenue, along with several Q-Park and NCP sites around the centre. For the Titanic Quarter, there are surface car parks near Titanic Belfast and the SSE Arena, while the Cathedral Quarter and St Anne's Square fill up quickly on weekend evenings. On-street pay-and-display bays are spread across the centre, and Belfast City Council runs a number of off-street car parks in the surrounding streets too.

If you're coming in from further out, the Park and Ride sites on the edge of the city, such as Cairnshill and Sprucefield, let you leave the car and continue by bus, saving both money and the stress of inner-ring traffic. Blue Badge holders can use designated bays and benefit from concessions, and many car parks offer cheaper evening and overnight tariffs. ParkEasy is free and community-powered, so local drivers share up-to-date tips on which spots fill early, where the cheaper options are, and where free on-street parking opens up after hours.

Belfast parking FAQs

Is there any free parking in Belfast city centre?

On-street pay-and-display bays are not free during controlled hours, but many become free in the evenings (usually after 6pm) and on Sundays. Some residential streets just outside the inner ring in south and east Belfast offer free parking if you don't mind a short walk. Always read the signs on the individual bay, as restrictions vary street by street.

What are the parking rules on Sundays and in the evenings?

Most council on-street pay-and-display bays are free on Sundays and outside controlled hours, which typically end around 6pm on weekdays and Saturdays. Multi-storey and private car parks still charge but often switch to lower evening or flat-rate tariffs. Check the tariff board before parking, and watch for loading bays and resident-permit areas that apply at all times.

Where should I avoid parking in Belfast?

Avoid stopping in clearway and bus lane sections along main routes like the Westlink and arterial roads such as the Lisburn Road, Ormeau Road and Antrim Road during peak hours, as these are enforced. Double yellow lines apply around the clock, and the area around the city hospital and Queen's University gets very congested on weekdays. For big events at the SSE Arena or Windsor Park, arrive early or use Park and Ride, as nearby streets fill fast.
