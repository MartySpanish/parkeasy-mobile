Parking in Portrush

Portrush is a busy seaside resort on the Causeway Coast, and parking demand swings hugely between a quiet winter weekday and a packed summer weekend. The main pay-and-display car parks are dotted around the town centre and seafront, including the large Lansdowne car park near the East Strand, the area around the harbour off Kerr Street and Harbour Road, and parking close to the train station and the Dunluce Centre. Smaller car parks and on-street bays run along Eglinton Street, Main Street, Mark Street and the Mark Street area, so it is usually worth driving a street or two back from the seafront where spaces turn over more often.

Free on-street parking does exist in the residential streets uphill and away from the promenade, but signed time limits and disc-zone style restrictions apply in parts of the centre, so always read the plate on the post before you leave the car. Beach lovers heading for the East Strand or West Strand will find the closest spaces fill first, especially on warm days and during events like the air show and golf weeks at Royal Portrush, when the whole town gets extremely tight. Portrush parking FAQs

Is there any free parking in Portrush?

Yes, but it is limited. Many seafront and town-centre bays are pay-and-display or time-restricted, while free spaces tend to be on residential streets a short walk uphill from the promenade. Always check the signs, as some apparently free streets still carry maximum-stay limits. Arriving early and parking a few streets back is usually your best chance of a free space in summer.

Do I have to pay for parking on Sundays and in the evenings?

Charging times vary by car park, and many pay-and-display areas in Portrush stop charging in the evenings and may have reduced or no charges on Sundays. The town stays busy on summer Sundays regardless, so spaces can still be scarce even when they are free. Check the tariff board at the specific car park, as times differ between council sites and the seafront bays.

Where should I avoid trying to park in Portrush?

Avoid circling the seafront and harbour at peak times, as the bays right beside the East Strand, West Strand and Kerr Street fill first and turnover is slow. The town becomes especially congested during the air show, golf events at Royal Portrush and warm summer weekends. Park further back near the Dunluce Centre, the train station or the residential streets, then walk down to the front.
