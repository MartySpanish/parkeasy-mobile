Parking in Newcastle

Newcastle sits where the Mourne Mountains sweep down to the sea, so parking demand swings hard with the weather and the seasons. The main pay-and-display options run along the seafront and just off Main Street, with the large Central Promenade car park and the Donard Park car park near Donard Forest being the busiest choices for beach days and walkers heading up Slieve Donard. On sunny weekends and bank holidays these fill early, so arriving before mid-morning makes a real difference.

Beyond the front you will find spaces around the town centre off Main Street and near the Newcastle Centre, plus council car parks dotted between the promenade and the harbour. Walkers often use the Donard car park or head a few minutes out to Tollymore Forest Park, which has its own pay-on-entry parking. ParkEasy is a free, community-powered app where local drivers share where they actually found a space, how busy each car park was, and which spots stay quiet, so you can plan before you arrive rather than circling the seafront.

Newcastle parking FAQs

Is there any free parking in Newcastle?

Most seafront and town-centre car parks are pay-and-display, so genuinely free spaces are limited and go quickly. Some on-street parking on residential roads set back from Main Street and the promenade can be free, but check the signage carefully for restrictions and time limits before leaving your car. Note that Tollymore and Donard forest parking is paid on entry.

What is parking like on sunny weekends and bank holidays?

Newcastle is one of the busiest day-trip spots in County Down, so the Central Promenade and Donard Park car parks often fill by late morning on warm weekends and holidays. Arrive early, or use ParkEasy to see which car parks other drivers report as still having space before you commit to the seafront.

Where should I avoid parking, and what about evenings and Sundays?

Avoid blocking residential side streets near the promenade and watch for double yellow lines along Main Street, where enforcement is active in season. In the evenings many pay-and-display car parks become free once charging hours end, and Sundays can be quieter than Saturdays, though good weather still brings crowds. Always read the on-site tariff board, as hours vary by car park.
