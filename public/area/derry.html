Parking in Derry~Londonderry

Derry~Londonderry is a compact walled city, so most visitors aim for the centre and the riverside. The biggest covered options are the multi-storey car parks at Foyleside Shopping Centre and Quayside Shopping Centre, both within a short walk of the historic walls, the Diamond and the Guildhall. Council-run car parks managed by Derry City and Strabane District Council are dotted around the centre too, including spaces near Foyle Street, William Street and the Strand Road area, while the Foyle Street side suits anyone arriving by coach at the bus station or crossing the Peace Bridge on foot.

On-street parking in the centre is mostly pay-and-display and time-limited during the day, with traffic wardens active, so check the signage on each street rather than assuming it is free. If you are happy to walk a little, the streets and car parks slightly out from the core, and the area across the river around Ebrington Square and the Waterside, tend to be quieter and easier to find a space. ParkEasy is free and community-powered, so locals and visitors share live tips on which car parks fill up, where the cheaper longer-stay bays are, and where parking is genuinely free at quieter times.

Derry~Londonderry parking FAQs

Where can I find free parking in Derry~Londonderry?

Free parking in the city centre is limited, but you can usually find it on residential streets a short walk out from the core and across the river around Ebrington and the Waterside, where many bays are unrestricted. Always read the signs on the day, as some streets are residents-only or time-limited. ParkEasy users flag the spots that are reliably free, so check the latest community tips before you set off.

Is parking free on Sundays and in the evenings?

Many council pay-and-display car parks and on-street bays in Derry~Londonderry stop charging in the evenings and on Sundays, so you can often park free once the daytime charging hours end. The exact times vary from street to street and car park to car park, so check the tariff board on the day. Private car parks like Foyleside and Quayside set their own hours, so confirm before relying on free evening parking there.

Where should I avoid parking in Derry~Londonderry?

Avoid leaving a car on time-limited centre streets for longer than the bay allows, as wardens patrol the core and overstaying can mean a ticket. The tight streets right inside the walls fill quickly and can be awkward to manoeuvre, so a multi-storey like Foyleside or Quayside is usually easier. Also steer clear of residents-only bays and any street with event or market restrictions, which are common near the Guildhall and Ebrington when something is on.
