Parking in Carrickfergus

Carrickfergus is an easy town to park in compared with central Belfast, and most of its car parks are free. The main town-centre car parks sit around the seafront and harbour, putting you within a short walk of Carrickfergus Castle, the marina, and the High Street and West Street shops. There is also parking close to the Carrickfergus Museum and the Town Hall, and the railway station has its own car park if you are catching the train into Belfast.

On-street spaces are available along streets such as High Street and West Street and around the town centre, though these are often limited-stay during the day to keep spaces turning over for shoppers. The seafront and castle area can get busy on sunny weekends and during events like the medieval fair, so arriving earlier is wise. Carrickfergus parking FAQs

Is parking free in Carrickfergus?

Yes, much of the parking in Carrickfergus is free, including the council car parks around the seafront, harbour and castle area. On-street parking is generally free too, but watch for limited-stay restrictions in the busiest town-centre streets during the day.

Where can I park for the castle and marina?

The car parks along the seafront near the harbour and marina are the most convenient for Carrickfergus Castle, and from there it is a short, flat walk to the castle entrance and the High Street shops. These fill up on warm weekends and during events, so try to arrive earlier in the day.

What about parking on Sundays and in the evenings?

Parking is at its quietest on Sunday mornings and in the evenings, when town-centre streets are far less busy. The free car parks remain free at these times, so the seafront and harbour spaces are usually a safe bet for an evening walk or a weekend visit to the castle.
